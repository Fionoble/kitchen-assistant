# Design System Strategy: The Digital Epicurean

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**Creative North Star: The Organic Editorial**
This design system moves away from the sterile, "tech-first" aesthetic of typical AI apps and moves toward the tactile, warm world of high-end culinary journalism. Our goal is to make the user feel like they are flipping through a bespoke food magazine that happens to be powered by intelligence.

We achieve this through **Intentional Asymmetry** and **Tonal Depth**. Instead of rigid, centered grids, we use overlapping elements—such as a recipe card partially bleeding over a header—to create a sense of movement. The interface should feel "grown," not "built," utilizing soft organic shapes and a hierarchy that prioritizes appetite appeal over data density.

---

## 2. Colors & Surface Philosophy
The palette is rooted in the earth: the deep `primary` (#154212) of a lush forest and the vibrant `tertiary` (#5d2c00 / carrot-inspired) of fresh produce, all resting on a "Soft Cream" `background` (#fbf9f5).

### The "No-Line" Rule
**Borders are prohibited for sectioning.** To define boundaries, we use background shifts. A recipe discovery section should transition from `surface` to `surface-container-low` to create a natural visual break. Lines are a sign of structural weakness; color transitions are a sign of intentional craft.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as physical layers of fine stationery.
*   **Base:** `surface` (#fbf9f5)
*   **Secondary Content:** `surface-container-low` (#f5f3ef)
*   **Interactive Cards:** `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff) to provide "pop" against the cream base.
*   **Overlays:** Use `surface-variant` with a 15% opacity and a 20px backdrop-blur to create a "Frosted Glass" effect for AI-generated suggestions, allowing the food photography to bleed through beautifully.

### Signature Textures
Avoid flat primary buttons. Apply a subtle linear gradient from `primary` (#154212) to `primary_container` (#2d5a27) at a 145-degree angle. This adds a "silk-screen" finish that feels premium and tactile.

---

## 3. Typography: The Friendly Professional
We pair the authority of a serif with the accessibility of a modern sans-serif to create a "Gourmet Guide" persona.

*   **The Hero (Display & Headline):** `notoSerif`. This is our editorial voice. Use `display-lg` for hero recipe titles. The serif's high contrast communicates "premium" and "curated."
*   **The Guide (Title & Body):** `plusJakartaSans`. This is our functional voice. It is highly legible and friendly. Use `body-lg` for recipe steps to ensure readability while cooking.
*   **The Detail (Label):** `plusJakartaSans`. Use `label-md` in `on_surface_variant` (#42493e) for metadata like "15 min" or "Easy," keeping the focus on the content.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth
Depth in this system is achieved through **Tonal Layering**, not heavy shadows.

*   **The Layering Principle:** To lift a card, do not reach for a shadow first. Place a `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff) card on top of a `surface-container` (#efeeea) background. This creates a soft, sophisticated "lift."
*   **Ambient Shadows:** When an element must float (e.g., a "Generate Recipe" FAB), use a shadow tinted with `primary` (#154212). 
    *   *Spec:* `0px 12px 32px rgba(21, 66, 18, 0.08)`. 
    *   This mimics natural light passing through a green glass bottle or forest canopy.
*   **The Ghost Border:** If high-contrast environments require a border, use `outline_variant` at 15% opacity. Never use 100% opaque borders.

---

## 5. Components

### The "Epicurean" Card
*   **Shape:** `xl` (1.5rem) rounded corners.
*   **Structure:** No dividers. Use `title-md` for the name and `body-sm` for the description, separated by 12px of vertical white space.
*   **AI Integration:** Recipes generated by AI should feature a subtle `tertiary_container` (#7f3f00) glow or a glassmorphic "AI Suggestion" badge in the corner.

### Buttons (CTAs)
*   **Primary:** Gradient of `primary` to `primary_container`. Text in `on_primary`. Shape: `full` (pill-shaped) for a friendly, approachable feel.
*   **Secondary:** `surface-container-high` background with `primary` text. No border.
*   **Tertiary (The "Carrot" Action):** Use `tertiary` (#5d2c00) for specialized AI actions like "Swap Ingredient."

### Input Fields & Search
*   **Styling:** `surface-container-low` background. No border. 
*   **Interaction:** On focus, transition the background to `surface-container-lowest` and add a 1px `primary` "Ghost Border" at 20% opacity.

### Lists & Ingredients
*   **Strict Rule:** No horizontal rules (lines) between ingredients. Use 8px of vertical spacing and a `surface-container-low` background on every second item to create a "zebra stripe" for legibility if the list is long.

---

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do
*   **DO** use high-quality, "messy" food photography. The AI should feel like it understands the soul of cooking, not just the chemistry.
*   **DO** use white space as a structural element. If a screen feels crowded, increase the padding to the `xl` scale.
*   **DO** overlap images. Let a photo of a carrot slightly overlap the header text for a high-end magazine feel.

### Don't
*   **DON'T** use pure black (#000000) for text. Use `on_surface` (#1b1c1a) to keep the "Cream and Forest" warmth.
*   **DON'T** use standard "Robot" icons for AI. Use organic sparkles or soft glowing pulses in `tertiary_fixed` (#ffdcc5) to represent "Intelligence."
*   **DON'T** use 1px dividers. If you need to separate content, use a 16px or 24px gap or a tonal shift.
