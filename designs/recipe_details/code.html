Honey-Glazed
Heirloom Carrots

An elevated take on earth's simple treasures. Slow-braised with star anise, wild honey, and finished with whipped goat cheese and toasted pistachios.

schedule 45 Mins
restaurant Serves 4

Ingredients (6 items)

  • check
    2 lbs Multi-colored Heirloom Carrots
  • check
    3 tbsp Wildflower Honey
  • check
    4 oz Soft Goat Cheese
  • check
    1/2 cup Shelled Pistachios, toasted
  • check
    2 Whole Star Anise pods
  • check
    Fresh Thyme sprigs for garnish

"The Digital Epicurean Tip: If you can't find heirloom, organic orange bunch carrots work beautifully if they have the greens still attached."

Preparation

1

Prepare the Carrots

Trim the greens from the carrots, leaving about 1 inch of the stem. Gently scrub the skins under cool water. If the carrots are very thick, slice them in half lengthwise to ensure even cooking.

2

The Aromatics & Honey

In a large wide skillet, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add the star anise and carrots. Toss to coat and cook for 3 minutes until slightly softened. Drizzle with the honey and a pinch of flaky sea salt.

3

Braise & Glaze

Add 1/4 cup of water or vegetable stock to the pan. Cover tightly and simmer for 10-12 minutes. Remove the lid, increase heat to medium-high, and cook until the liquid reduces to a thick, syrupy glaze that clings to the carrots.

4

The Final Touch

Transfer to a serving platter. While still warm, crumble the goat cheese over the top and sprinkle with toasted pistachios. Finish with fresh thyme and a crack of black pepper.
